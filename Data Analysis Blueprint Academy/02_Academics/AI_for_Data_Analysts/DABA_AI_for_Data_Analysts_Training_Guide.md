# DABA AI for Data Analysts Training Guide

**Document Code:** DABA-AI-002  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Enterprise Simulation:** ABC Retail Ltd  
**Founder:** Mbah Dousbel Angum  

## Module purpose

This module teaches learners to use generative AI as a controlled analytical assistant. Students learn how to formulate prompts, evaluate outputs, protect data, validate calculations, explain technical work, generate business insights, and produce management-ready communication.

The analyst remains accountable for every result.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of the module, students should be able to:

- Explain the role and limitations of generative AI in data analysis.
- Write structured prompts for analytical tasks.
- Use AI to support Excel, SQL, Power BI, and reporting work.
- Validate formulas, queries, calculations, and written conclusions.
- Distinguish facts from assumptions and recommendations.
- Identify hallucination, privacy, bias, copyright, and overreliance risks.
- Maintain an evidence trail of prompts, outputs, checks, and corrections.
- Complete an AI-assisted business analysis without surrendering professional judgment.

## Eight-session structure

### Session 1 — AI foundations for analysts

Topics:

- Generative AI versus traditional analytics.
- What AI can and cannot do.
- The role of human judgment.
- Examples of useful and unsafe AI use.

Practical:

Students test an AI assistant using four types of analytics:

- Descriptive
- Diagnostic
- Predictive
- Prescriptive

### Session 2 — Analytical prompt engineering

Teach the DABA prompt structure:

1. Role
2. Context
3. Task
4. Data
5. Output
6. Constraints
7. Validation

Students rewrite weak prompts into professional analytical prompts.

### Session 3 — AI-assisted data cleaning

Students use fictional messy customer or employee data to ask AI for:

- Duplicate detection ideas.
- Missing-value analysis.
- Standardization rules.
- Validation checks.
- Cleaning documentation.

AI recommendations must be checked manually before implementation.

### Session 4 — Excel, SQL, and Power BI assistance

Students ask AI to:

- Explain formulas.
- Debug formulas.
- Draft SQL queries.
- Explain joins.
- Suggest DAX measures.
- Recommend dashboard visuals.

Every technical output must be tested in the relevant application.

### Session 5 — Insight generation and validation

Students provide approved KPI results and ask AI to generate findings.

They then classify each statement as:

- Verified fact
- Interpretation
- Assumption
- Recommendation
- Unsupported claim

### Session 6 — Data storytelling and reporting

Students use AI to draft:

- Executive summaries.
- Findings.
- Recommendations.
- Presentation talking points.
- Technical explanations for non-technical managers.

The final version must be edited for accuracy, tone, and organizational context.

### Session 7 — Responsible AI

Topics:

- Hallucination
- Privacy and confidentiality
- Bias and fairness
- Copyright
- Security
- Overreliance
- Transparency
- Human oversight

Students complete the AI Risk Register and Validation Log.

### Session 8 — ABC Retail Ltd capstone

Students analyze validated ABC Retail Ltd KPIs and produce:

- Five findings.
- Three recommendations.
- One operational risk.
- One data limitation.
- A management summary.
- A complete prompt and validation evidence pack.

## Instructor rules

The instructor should require students to:

- Preserve the original prompt.
- Preserve the first AI output.
- Document corrections.
- Cite the internal data source or worksheet used.
- Explain the final answer in their own words.
- Avoid uploading real personal or confidential data.
- Test technical outputs before submission.
- Declare material AI use in assessed work.

## Recommended AI-use classification

### Allowed

- Brainstorming.
- Explaining concepts.
- Drafting formulas or queries.
- Structuring reports.
- Generating practice questions.
- Improving clarity and grammar.
- Producing alternative visual ideas.

### Allowed with disclosure and validation

- Generating business insights.
- Drafting management recommendations.
- Creating code or formulas used in assessment.
- Summarizing source data.
- Producing charts or analytical narratives.

### Prohibited

- Uploading restricted or confidential information into an unapproved service.
- Submitting AI output without understanding it.
- Fabricating sources or evidence.
- Using AI during a closed assessment where assistance is prohibited.
- Hiding material AI use when disclosure is required.
- Using AI to impersonate another person.

## Final evidence pack

Each learner submits:

1. Original business question.
2. Source data reference.
3. Final structured prompt.
4. Initial AI output.
5. Validation log.
6. Corrections made.
7. Final approved analysis.
8. Reflection on what the AI did well and poorly.
