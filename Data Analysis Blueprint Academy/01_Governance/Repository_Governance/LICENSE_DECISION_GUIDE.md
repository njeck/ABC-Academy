# Licence Decision Guide

The repository currently has no approved reuse licence. A licence must be selected intentionally.

## Option 1 — All rights reserved

Best when:

- The materials are proprietary academy products.
- Commercial reuse should require written approval.
- Instructor resources and assessments must be tightly controlled.

Action:

- Add a copyright notice and formal terms of use.
- Keep restricted materials private.

## Option 2 — Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives

Best when:

- Public reading and sharing are allowed.
- Commercial reuse and modification should be restricted.

Limitation:

- The no-derivatives condition may reduce educational collaboration.

## Option 3 — Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike

Best when:

- Non-commercial adaptation is welcome.
- Derivative educational work must remain under the same terms.

## Recommendation

Use a split model:

- Public educational samples: an approved Creative Commons licence.
- Proprietary curriculum, assessments, instructor keys, and academy operations: all rights reserved in a private repository.

Legal review is recommended before publishing the final licence text.
